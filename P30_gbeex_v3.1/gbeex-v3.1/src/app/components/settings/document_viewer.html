Protocol: CardioHealth Study (CHS-01)

Version 2.1 - Last Updated: June 15, 2025

1.0 Introduction

This document outlines the protocol for a multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study to evaluate the efficacy and safety of Cardiocare (Codenamed RX-123) in patients with moderate to severe heart failure. The study aims to enroll approximately 500 subjects across 25 sites globally.

The primary objective is to assess the effect of Cardiocare on the composite endpoint of cardiovascular death or hospitalization for heart failure.

2.0 Objectives

The primary and secondary objectives of this study are as follows:

  • To evaluate the effect of Cardiocare compared with placebo on the primary composite endpoint.
  • To assess the safety and tolerability profile of Cardiocare.
  • To evaluate the effect of Cardiocare on patient-reported outcomes using the Kansas City Cardiomyopathy Questionnaire (KCCQ).

4.0 Eligibility Criteria

Subjects must meet all of the following inclusion criteria to be eligible for enrollment into the study:

4.2 Exclusion Criteria

Subjects who meet any of the following exclusion criteria are not eligible for enrollment:

  1. Myocardial infarction within the last 3 months.
  2. Systolic blood pressure > 160 mmHg or < 90 mmHg.
  3. History of significant renal or hepatic impairment.
